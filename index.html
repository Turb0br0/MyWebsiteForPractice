My Mission

Hello, and welcome to my list of open-source apps that I want to bring to everyone's attention. It is important to me that the internet does not change for the worse and that we avoid corporate dominance. I hope to motivate you to contribute to open source and keep things free and accessible for everyone. If that doesn't resonate with you, I hope to inspire you to create your own project and learn from it. Any small contribution to the internet that isn't profit-motivated is a win for us all.

As technology advances and corporations grow, it becomes increasingly difficult for the average user to take control of their online lives. This gives big tech an advantage and allows them to test how far they can push the public into accepting unfair practices. I encourage people to refrain from doing business with companies that do not operate fairly, such as what Nintendo is doing with the Switch 2. I believe that many people tolerate Nintendo and purchase the Switch 2 due to emotional attachment, but I recognize that I am just one voice in all this.

Now back to the purpose of this site. This video will cover some actions you can take to utilize open and free software. Louis Rossmann has a bunch of videos explaining how the internet is changing. Also Notice the video is from Odysee, an American decentralized video hosting platform. A good alternative that isn't using you.

Odysee

Odysee is an American decentralized video hosting platform, built on the LBRY blockchain. It positions itself as an alternative to mainstream services like YouTube, but with a focus on freedom of speech and decentralization.
